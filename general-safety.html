General Safety Tips

Winter conditions can be harsh and unpredictable. This page covers prevention tips and first aid measures to help you stay safe.

Prevention Tips for Cold Weather

Staying warm and prepared is key to avoiding cold-related dangers. Follow these tips to protect yourself from extreme temperatures and harsh conditions.

Dress in Layers

Wear moisture-wicking, insulating, and windproof layers.

Check the Weather

Avoid going out in severe cold, blizzards, or high avalanche risk.

Carry Emergency Gear

Flashlight, whistle, hand warmers, and a thermal blanket.

Keep Extremities Warm

Use insulated gloves, hats, socks, and boots.

Limit Exposure Time

Take breaks indoors or in sheltered areas.

Stay Hydrated & Eat High-Energy Foods

Your body needs fuel to stay warm.

Stay Dry

Wet clothes increase heat loss; pack extra socks and gloves.

Use the Buddy System

Never venture alone into extreme conditions.

First Aid & Emergency Response

Staying warm and prepared is key to avoiding cold-related dangers. Follow these tips to protect yourself from extreme temperatures and harsh conditions.

STEP 1

Assess & Move to Safety

  • Check if the person is conscious, breathing, and responsive.
  • Look for signs of hypothermia or frostbite.
  • Move them out of the wind and into shelter.

STEP 2

Provide Basic First Aid

  • Warm them up gradually (no direct heat!).
  • Cover frostbitten areas & avoid rubbing.
  • If conscious, give warm fluids.

STEP 3

Call for Help

  • Use a satellite phone, whistle, or emergency beacon.
  • Contact mountain rescue or ski patrol.
  • Stay with the injured person until help arrives.
