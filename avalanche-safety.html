Avalanche Safety

Avalanches are powerful snow slides that can occur suddenly, posing a serious risk to anyone in the mountains. Knowing what causes them and how to stay safe is essential for every adventurer. This page covers key avalanche dangers and the best ways to protect yourself.

What Causes Avalanches?

Understanding avalanches can save lives.

Natural Triggers Human-Caused

Heavy snowfall, temperature shifts, strong winds, or earthquakes can weaken the snowpack, causing it to slide. Recognizing these conditions helps adventurers assess risks.

Heavy Snowfall

Sudden snow accumulation can overload weak layers.

Temperature Fluctuations

Warming can make snow layers unstable, increasing the risk.

Wind & Rain

Wind can move snow to form unstable slabs, and rain can weaken layers.

Earthquakes & Other Natural Factors

Tremors, falling rocks, or tree collapses can trigger slides.

How to Stay Safe ?

Avalanches are unpredictable, but preparation reduces risks. Learn key safety measures, from checking forecasts to carrying essential gear.

Before You Go During an Avalanche After an Avalanche

Before You Go: Prepare & Prevent

Preparation is key to staying safe in avalanche terrain. Check conditions, pack essential gear, and plan your route before heading out.

  • Check Avalanche Forecasts: Always look at our live forecast before going.
  • Carry Essential Gear: Beacon, probe, shovel, airbag pack.
  • Take an Avalanche Safety Course: Knowing what to do can save lives. We offer all kinds of training and events.
  • Plan Your Route Wisely: Avoid slopes steeper than 30 degrees in risky conditions.

During an Avalanche: What to Do

Stay calm and act fast. Try to move to the side, ditch heavy gear, and protect your airway. If caught, create space to breathe and signal for help.

Try to Escape

Move sideways or grab onto something stable.

Ditch Heavy Gear

Reduces weight and increases mobility.

"Swim" & Stay on Top

Keep moving to avoid being buried deep.

Protect Your Airway

Cup hands over your mouth to create breathing space.

After an Avalanche: Rescue & Survival

Act quickly. Call for help, search for buried companions, and use a beacon if available. Stay aware of further risks and wait for rescue if needed.

If You’re Buried:

Stay calm, try to move an arm upward to create space.

If You See Someone Buried:

Use transceiver & probe to locate them.

Call for Emergency Help:

Provide GPS coordinates if possible.
